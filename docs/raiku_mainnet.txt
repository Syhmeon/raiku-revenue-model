Raiku Private Mainnet Introduction
Executive Summary
Solana processes billions in trading volume daily, yet transaction inclusion remains fundamentally probabilistic. Bot transactions experience ~58% failure rates under normal conditions. During high contention, failure rates exceed 75%. For professional traders, this is a systematic drag on P&L.
The cost of unreliable execution compounds: infrastructure spend on retries, wasted priority fees, stale quotes leading to adverse selection, and conservative position sizing because you don't know if your hedge will land. When HumidiFi's execution reliability degraded, their PropAMM market share collapsed from 26% to <1% in just 10 weeks. The market is unforgiving.
Raiku exists to make execution on Solana predictable under real economic load. For professional traders, the dominant failure mode on Solana isn't latency or TPS, but uncertainty around transaction inclusion and ordering.
Why Existing TX Delivery is Unreliable
The hidden cost: Not just failed transactions, but everything spent overcoming uncertainty e.g. redundant RPCs, co-located infrastructure, retry logic and processes, network spam from concurrent attempts, and conservative position sizing because you don't know if your hedge will land.
Raiku's Solution
Raiku's value isn't about being cheaper or faster than Jito bundles. It's about removing inclusion uncertainty so that your existing speed advantages actually convert to wins.
Current solutions optimize how you compete in real-time auctions. Raiku lets you exit the auction entirely and reserve capacity ahead of time. The competition shifts from "Who can get included first" to "Who can detect and build the transaction first"; a race sophisticated traders consistently win.
AOT Auction (Ahead-of-Time)
Today, decision-making and execution are coupled. When you detect an opportunity, you build the transaction and submit; then hope it lands. If the network is congested at the exact moment you need to act, you're exposed. Your edge in detection means nothing if you lose the inclusion lottery.
AOT decouples decision-making from execution risk. You reserve blockspace before you know what you'll need it for. When an opportunity appears, you already have guaranteed capacity waiting. You're not racing for inclusion; you're racing to detect and build fastest.
This mirrors how execution works in TradFi. Prime brokerage relationships, co-location, guaranteed clearing; the infrastructure is pre-arranged. You're not scrambling for access when you want to trade. AOT brings that model to Solana.
How It Works
For every slot where a Raiku validator is leader, compute units (CUs) are sold ahead of time via sealed-bid auction. Bids remain private until settlement. Winners receive guaranteed blockspace reservation in future slots.
Auction Mechanics
Auctions open ~1 hour before the target slot (configurable)
Bidding closes a few slots before execution
Winners notified before the slot begins
Requires SOL deposit to participate
Execution Guarantees
Early submission → guaranteed top-of-block position
Late submission → included as soon as possible
AOT transactions land before all external transactions
Predictable, forecastable costs with no real-time tip competition
Important Limitations
AOT Guarantees:
Slot-level inclusion commitment for reserved CUs
Top-of-block positioning for early submissions
Predictable, projectable costs
Elimination of real-time priority competition
AOT Does Not Guarantee:
Transaction profitability: your transaction can still fail if built against stale state or with insufficient slippage tolerance
Zero breach risk: leader breaches are penalized but not prevented
Execution success: P(Execution) remains ~0.95, dependent on transaction validity
Important: "Guaranteed inclusion" means slot-level inclusion commitment, not guaranteed profit. Sophisticated traders should model AOT as dramatically improved P(Delivery) and P(Scheduling), with P(Execution) unchanged.
